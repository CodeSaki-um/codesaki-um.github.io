Sam Aguilar's Guide to Proper Plant Keeping

Having a little leaf-baby can be hard work! Looking into the best

liquids to give them can save you some of that [work] giving you

some much deserved free time. Why do you care about the drink

you give your plants? Imagine being super thirsty on a hot day, and

someone gives you warm dirty water, yuck! You want those green

little guys to be healthy and happy in your home, don't 'cha?!

Well, today you are going to see which drink benefits your plant.

Water

Humans are made of mainly water and so is the planet we inhabit.

It is normal to assume that plain water is the best option when

caring for your plants. However, there are other options you can

utilize to improve your plant's overall quality of life. Water's

a great starting point, but filters and tubing can remove or add

many minerals to the water. This will end up damaging the plant

either way because it can intake harmful minerals, or not absorb

enough of them. Plain water can be improved upon, but one major

misconception many have, is that adding sugar helps your plants.

Sugar can alter the way your plants absorb water/nutrients, in

turn; this can cause healthy plants to start declining in health.

Rice Water

On the other hand, rice water is a step up from plain/sugar

water. Dry rice is packed with many nutrients that is washed away

when rinsed for consumption. Next time you find yourself washing a

portion of rice, keep the water and watch how great your plants will

look in no time. Your babies will be thanking you, try it out, see

for yourself!

Milk

Milk might seem a little out of place, but today, crazy ideas are a

new trend people like following. For the record, the nutrients milk

has is barely beneficial or even accessible to human bodies let alone

plants. To reiterate, your little leaf-babies do not want to drink

milk...ever.

A few alternatives for your leaf-babies:

  • Coffee Grounds
  • Banana Water
  • Oatmeal Water

These are a few options if rice water is not easily accessible
